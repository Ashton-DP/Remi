The problem

Chasing money is the worst part of the job.

It's awkward, it's easy to forget, and every invoice you don't follow up on is cash sitting in someone else's account. So most businesses just… don't — and quietly write the money off. PaidUp does the chasing for you, so you never have to send another "just following up" message again.

How it works

A polite, persistent follow-up sequence.

Remi escalates gently over days — warm at first, firmer over time, never rude — and stops the instant the invoice is paid.

1
Due date · friendly heads-up
"Hi Jane 👋 Just a reminder that invoice #1042 (R2,400) is due today. You can settle it in one tap here: pay.remi.co/1042"
2
Day 3 · gentle nudge
"Hi Jane, following up on invoice #1042 (R2,400) — it's a few days overdue. Here's the link again: pay.remi.co/1042"
3
Day 7 · firm reminder
"Hi Jane, invoice #1042 (R2,400) is now a week overdue. Please settle it when you can: pay.remi.co/1042"
4
Day 14 · final notice
"Hi Jane, this is a final reminder for invoice #1042 (R2,400). Please arrange payment, or reply here if there's a problem: pay.remi.co/1042"
✓
Paid · chase stops
✅ Payment received — invoice #1042 settled. Remi stops chasing automatically and sends a thank-you.

Timing and tone are configurable. Every message goes over WhatsApp first, with email as backup.

It handles the replies

When they reply, Remi reacts — not a dumb auto-sender.

💸

"I already paid"

Remi pauses the chase and flags it for you to confirm — no nagging someone who's already settled.

⚖️

"I dispute this"

Chasing stops immediately and the invoice is marked disputed for you to look at — never argue with a customer by accident.

⏳

"Can I pay next week?"

Remi snoozes the follow-ups to the date they ask for, then quietly resumes.

🛑

"STOP"

Instant opt-out, honoured automatically — POPIA-friendly, and it protects your number's reputation.
